Is crime in New York really that bad? Murder crime statistics say otherwise.

A look into data reported by the NYPD shows a decrease in murder precinct records from 2000 to 2021.

BY ARVELISSE BONILLA RAMOS

DECEMBER 19, 2022 (Image – Shutterstock)


Data reported by the New York City Police Department (NYPD) from 2000 through 2021 show a significant decline in the number of homicides with a crime percentage decrease of 27% across all boroughs. In 2000, 673 homicides were reported by precincts – in the Bronx, Staten Island, Manhattan, Queens and Brooklyn – compared to 488 in 2021. Murder and non-negligent manslaughter is considered one of the seven major felony offenses alongside: rape, robbery, felony assault, burglary, grand larceny and grand larceny of motor vehicle.

The data does show that a handful of precincts saw an increase in the number of murders and non-negligent manslaughter recorded and reported in 2021 compared to 2000. There are 77 police precincts.



MURDER RATES DOWN

The 52nd Precinct saw a decrease of 75% of reported homicide cases. In 2020 there were 25 cases recorded versus 7 in 2021. The precinct serves Bedford Park, Fordham, Kingsbridge, Norwood, Bronx Park, and University Heights – all in the Bronx.

The 46th Precinct serves the central part of the western Bronx. It saw a decrease of 48% points, with 23 homicides recorded in 2020 and 12 in 2021. The neighborhoods include: Fordham, University Heights, Morris Heights and Mount Hope.

The 71st Precinct, located in central Brooklyn, contains the southern portion of Crown Heights, Wingate, and Prospect Lefferts. It saw a decrease by 83%, with 12 cases recorded and reported in 2020 and 2 in 2021. With 8 cases reported to date in this year so far.

The 33rd Precinct serves the Washington Heights section of northern Manhattan. This precinct saw a -58% decrease in murder statistics. With 12 murders reported in 2000 compared to 5 in 2021.

Precincts 30 and 90 also saw a decrease. The 30th precinct in Harlem (neighborhoods covered: Hamilton Heights, Sugar Hill, and West Harlem) reported 9 murders in 2000 compared to 3 in 2021. The 90th precinct reported 12 cases in 2000 compared to 5 in 2021. The precinct covers the neighborhood of Williamsburg – this is a -58% decrease.

MURDER RATES UP

One of these is the 73rd Precinct in northeast Brooklyn which serves Brownsville and Ocean Hill. The NYPD saw a rise in this precinct by 77% – 13 in 2020 versus 23 in 2021. To this date, 22 murders have been recorded in this precinct.

The 41st Precinct, which serves Hunts Point and Longwood in the Bronx, shows a 120% increase in cases. In 2020, 5 cases were reported compared to 11 in 2021.

HIGH NUMBERS

The 75th Precinct serves the easternmost portion of Brooklyn and envelopes East New York and Cypress Hills. Even though there is a decrease of 38% in this precinct. Both years number were high – with 40 murders recorded in 2020 and 25 in 2021. Grand Concourse, Bronx Terminal Market, and Yankee Stadium are served by the 44th Precinct and in 2000 there were 25 cases reported and 22 in 2021 (12% decrease).

The 47th Precinct serves the north Bronx including: Woodlawn, Wakefield, Williamsbridge, Baychester, Edenwald, Olinville, Fishbay, and Woodlawn Cemetery. Although the area saw a 45% decrease in cases reported and recorded, in 2021 there were 16 cases reported. To this date there have been 14 cases reported so far.

Data shows that the 43rd Precinct in the Bronx did not see a change as well in cases recorded, numbers are also high, with 16 cases reported both years (2020-2021). The precinct serves the southeast section of the Bronx. Including four primary commercial strips in the precinct: Westchester Avenue, Castle Hill Avenue, White Plains Road, and Parkchester.



MORE RECENT

According to the New York Times, NYPD officers are leaving the force for better financial pay and perks, stating that more than 9,400 officers have left since January 2000. The NYPD’s latest city crime statistics for November 2022 saw an overall index of crime in New York City decreased by 1.2% compared with November 2021 (10,196 versus 10,323).

Three of the seven major index-crime categories saw decreases, driven by a 14.1% decrease in rape, a 6.0% decrease in burglary, and a 5.5% decrease in grand larceny. There were 30 murders reported in November 2022 compared to 25 November 2021 for a +20.0% increase.
